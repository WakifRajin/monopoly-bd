Gameplay Rules

1. Setup, Turn Order, and Win Condition

  • Offline games support 2 to 8 players (any mix of human and AI). Online games require at least 2 ready human players, and only the host can launch.
  • Turn order follows lobby order and loops to the next non-bankrupt player each turn.
  • You start with the lobby starting-money value. If that value is invalid, the selected board theme default is used.
  • The selected board theme controls currency display, location names, GO salary amount, and default starting money.
  • Goal: make all other players bankrupt. The last non-bankrupt player wins.

2. Turn Phases and Dice Rules

  • Every turn runs in phases: Roll → Action/Land Result → End.
  • You must roll before you can end your turn.
  • If you roll doubles (and are not in jail), you get another roll after land effects resolve.
  • If you roll 3 doubles in one turn, you are sent directly to Jail immediately.
  • Movement wraps around 40 spaces. Passing GO pays the theme GO salary. Landing exactly on GO also pays salary.

3. Space Rules

  • GO: landing on GO pays salary.
  • Jail/Visit: safe when just visiting.
  • Free Parking: visiting only, no jackpot and no reward pool payout.
  • Go to Jail: sends you to Jail space (position 10), ends movement, and resets doubles streak.
  • Tax spaces: pay the printed amount to the bank.
  • Chance/Community Chest: draw top card from a shuffled deck and apply its action.

4. Buying, Auction, and Rent

  • Landing on an unowned property/railroad/utility lets you buy it at face price.
  • If you cannot afford a landed property, buy is disabled and you can auction it or skip.
  • Auction includes all non-bankrupt players. You can toggle auctions on/off in lobby settings. Bid controls are +100, +200, +500, +1000, or Pass.
  • When enabled, auctions start from an opening value (about 60%-80% of base property price), not from 0.
  • You cannot place a bid above your current cash. Auction ends when only one non-passed bidder remains with a valid high bid, or everyone passes.
  • If all pass, the property remains unsold (owner stays none).
  • Rent on normal properties: base rent, or double base if color monopoly and no buildings, or house/hotel rent tiers from the deed.
  • Railroad rent depends on owner total railroads. Inflated boards use 250 / 500 / 1000 / 2000; Classic uses 25 / 50 / 100 / 200.
  • Utility rent is dice-based. Most boards use inflated economy (40x one utility, 100x both); Classic board uses original multipliers (4x and 10x).
  • Mortgaged assets collect no rent.

5. Building and Mortgage Rules

  • You can build only after owning all properties in a color group.
  • Build cost per click is that property house cost.
  • At 4 houses, the next build converts that property to a hotel.
  • Selling buildings returns 50% of one house cost per sell action.
  • Selling a hotel converts it back to 4 houses first in this build.
  • Even-building distribution across a color set is not enforced by this build.
  • You can mortgage owned assets (property, railroad, utility) for 50% of purchase price, if that asset has no buildings.
  • You cannot mortgage a color property while any property in that same color group still has buildings.
  • Unmortgage cost is 110% of mortgage value (10% interest).

6. Jail Rules

  • You can be jailed by Go to Jail square, jail-type cards, or rolling 3 doubles in one turn.
  • When your jailed turn starts, choose to roll for doubles or leave immediately by paying bail/using a card.
  • Bail follows board economy: 500 on inflated boards, 50 on Classic, unless you use a Get Out of Jail Free card.
  • If you roll doubles in jail, you are released and move by that roll.
  • If you fail doubles, jail turn count increases. On the 3rd failed roll, you must pay bail, then move by that roll.

7. Chance and Community Actions (Implemented Set)

  • Chance deck actions include movement cards (GO, nearest railway, key destinations), jail cards, fines, rewards, and repair bills. Card values follow board economy: Classic uses original values, other boards use inflated equivalents.
  • Community Chest includes money gains/losses, GO/Jail moves, jail-free card, and birthday collection from each non-bankrupt opponent, with values scaled by board economy.
  • Get Out of Jail Free cards increase your stored jail-free card count.
  • Decks are shuffled at game start and drawn in order; when index exceeds length, drawing wraps back through the same shuffled deck order.

8. Debt, Bankruptcy, and Asset Transfer

  • If a human player cannot pay a charge, a debt prompt appears. You must raise money (sell buildings/mortgage) or declare bankruptcy.
  • You cannot end your turn while unresolved debt prompt is active.
  • If debt is paid after raising funds, the exact owed amount is transferred to the creditor (or bank) and play continues.
  • Bankruptcy to another player transfers remaining cash and all owned assets to that creditor.
  • If transferred assets are mortgaged, the creditor pays 10% mortgage-interest to the bank for those assets.
  • Bankruptcy to bank clears ownership, removes all buildings/hotel/mortgage status, and queues each asset for bank auction.
  • Bank auctions run in sequence until queue is empty.

9. Trading and Timer Rules

  • Trades can include properties and cash from both sides.
  • A property cannot be traded if it has buildings, or if any property in its same color group has buildings.
  • Recipient can accept or decline. Proposer can cancel while pending.
  • Trade is validated again on response (ownership and available cash must still be valid).
  • Auto-advance timer (Off / 10 / 20 / 30 / 45 / 60 seconds) runs only during end phase after movement resolves.
  • Timer pauses while any modal overlay is open. At zero, turn auto-ends.
  • Online mode rules: only host can launch match or change lobby timer/settings, and all connected players must be ready before start.
  • If someone leaves an online match, leave handling can convert them to AI control or liquidate and auction their assets (host-managed room state).
